EMD SPD45 is a group of escaped test subjects from a laboratory in Africa. They left and sacrificed their home for a better future. This group contains empty beings, never being able to experience being fully human and having an identity. The band name is made up of their labels given to them at birth, EM-45, DS-45 and PD-45. They escaped the lab when they were young adults, and came from a monochromatic underground world into a colorful world on the surface. They traveled around their new surroundings, going farther and farther each day until they found a modern humanity. With this new humanity found, there was new music to be found. These subjects were exposed to different cultures in the form of music. With more exposure overtime, they began to experiment with different types of music ranging from classical music to metal. These test subjects eventually gravitated to bluegrass, a type of music. They decided to sacrifice their other music interests to combine all three interests of each-other to strengthen their bond. As their own interests were discarded, so were their morals. They began to interact violently with the creatures of this world, only stopping to acknowledge them through sacrifices.
